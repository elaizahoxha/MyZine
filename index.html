My Zine

A mixed-media study of vulnerability, identity and relational care.

Visual Design · Narrative Interaction · Collaboration

scroll

Concept & Context

Softness challenges the belief that vulnerability is weakness. Through collage, physical artifacts, memory, and interaction, I explored how design can communicate gentleness — not through fragility, but as relational intelligence.

“Softness becomes strength when it gives us space to be seen.”

This project emerged from class explorations of boundaries, care, and identity. I wanted to create a space where people could pause and feel held — without needing to perform or impress. The zine format helped me explore personal stories while designing with honesty and emotional grounding.

Design Process

My process began with physical making. Cutting, taping, layering and discovering meaning through touch. Rather than designing what I thought would look “right,” I created artifacts that reflected how I experience softness in my real life: as memory, care, and resilience.

I also designed with vulnerability. Sharing pieces with teammates, receiving edits, and learning to let others impact my work. This expanded the emotional weight of the final outcome.

  • Physical collage → texture, imperfection
  • Digital adaptation → accessibility + longevity
  • Group synthesis → merging perspectives
  • Presentation refinement → communication through narrative UX

Visual Design System

Materiality

Collage scraps, paper grain, thread, and photographed textures ground the zine in touch and memory, reflecting honesty and vulnerability.

Soft Structure

Layouts with generous margins and gentle spacing create emotional safety, mirroring class themes of boundaries and relational care.

Emotional Palette

Cream, champagne gold, and muted greens evoke warmth, reflection, and grounding, supporting the intimate tone of the narrative.

Typographic Voice

A serif with personality paired with a soft sans expresses identity, confidence, and vulnerability through quiet contrast.

Mixed-Media Storytelling

Physical and digital layers—photos, scans, drawings, and textures—blend into a narrative that connects memory and identity.

Pace & Movement

Slow fades and intentional pacing guide viewers to pause, breathe, and engage emotionally, echoing the feel of a handmade zine.

Reflection

This project helped me understand how collaboration shapes product quality.

Working individually first gave us a foundation, but the real progress happened when we developed trust and felt safe revisiting each other’s ideas. As our comfort level increased, our feedback became more honest and our decisions became more aligned. I learned that successful design depends on more than technical execution—it requires communication, shared context, and a team environment where people feel confident contributing. Small details in our collages became points of alignment, helping us clarify intent and build a more cohesive final experience. The process made me appreciate iteration not just as a method, but as a mindset.

Outcomes

  • Led the visual and interaction direction, aligning the team around a shared design vision.
  • Established a collaborative workflow grounded in psychological safety, which improved feedback quality and decision-making speed.
  • Unified physical and digital artifacts into a coherent product narrative that strengthened the project's overall clarity and intent.
  • Improved narrative UX by restructuring the zine’s flow, increasing readability and emotional resonance.
  • Strengthened team alignment through open communication and structured critique, resulting in a more cohesive final outcome.
